Abdominal crunch

Abdominal crunches are designed to tone the core muscles of the body. 
The exercise aids in strengthening the core muscles, 
improving the posture, and increasing the mobility and flexibility of the muscles.
When these exercises are performed, the rectus abdominus and the oblique muscles are tightened.

Jumping jack 

 Jumping jacks work all the major muscles in the lower body,
 strengthening your glutes, hamstrings, quads, hips, calves, and shins. 
 The higher and faster you jump, the more power and strength you'll develop. 
 You can also wear a weighted vest to increase the intensity and load.

Bird dog

The bird dog is a simple core exercise that improves stability, 
encourages a neutral spine, and relieves low back pain. 
This exercise pose uses the whole body to target and strengthen your core, hips,
and back muscles. It also helps promote proper posture and increase range of motion.

Kneeling push up 

 Knee push-ups can improve upper-body strength. 
 The knee push-up activates muscle groups throughout your upper body,
 including your triceps, pecs, and shoulders.
 Knee push-ups can increase core stability. ...
 Knee push-ups are a beginner-level bodyweight exercise

High knees

High knees activate your quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, glutes, 
and hip flexors, helping improve muscular endurance, balance, and coordination in these muscles.

time cable pushdown

The tricep pushdown is one of the best exercises for tricep development .
While the versatile upper-body workout is usually done on a cable machine (a fixture at most gyms), 
you can also perform a version of the move at home or on the go using a resistant band.

Barbell deadlift

 Deadlifting can increase core strength, core stability and improve your posture. 
 Deadlifting trains most of the muscles in the legs, lower back and core. 
 These are all muscles responsible for posture, which will help keep your shoulders, spine, and hips in alignment.

seated cable row

Seated cable rows increase upper-body strength by activating multiple muscle groups throughou thebody,
including back muscles like the latissimus dorsi in your middle back, the erector spinea muscles
the rhomboids in your upper back, and the lower trapezius.

Plank jacks

 Plank jacks are a combined cardio and core-strengthening exercise.
 They can help you strengthen the muscles of both the upper and lower body.
 Adding plank jacks to your exercise routine a few times a week may also increase core strength and stability,
 burn calories, and help reduce fat.

back extension

 Back extension exercises improve the stability and strength of your erector spinae muscles. 
 They also improve the range of motion of your lower back and 
 can have a rehabilitative effect on people with poor lumbar or thoracic back posture.

kettlebell one leg deadlift

 The single leg deadlift strengthens the posterior chain namely the glutes,
 hamstrings, lower back and core muscles. Your balance and movement skills will also be improved with this exercise.

Wide stance

 Wider stances may be of benefit in allowing individuals to attain deeper squats without
 compromising ankle health. This is because in a wider squatting pattern, 
 the posterior tracking of the hips places the tibia more perpendicular to the ankle, thus requiring less ankle dorsiflexion.

Plank row

 The plank strengthens your spine, your rhomboids and trapezius, 
 and your abdominal muscles, which naturally result in a strong posture as they grow in strength.
 Developing your posture can improve on a number of ailments, and prevent the onset of other ones.
 Good posture means you're keeping your bones aligned.

Leg pull in knees up 

 Knee ups help strengthen the abdominal muscles, which are part of your core. 
 You can perform the knee up individually, add it to a resistance training session, 
 or include it in a comprehensive core workout.

dumbbells squat thrusters

 Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart with dumbbells above your shoulders.
 Squat down keeping your back flat and your knees over your toes. 
 Push through your heels to return to standing while pressing the dumbbells overhead with arms fully
 extended. 
Return to starting position and repeat.

Dumbbel crunch

Weighted crunches intensify the movement and 
deliver a powerful core exercise by targeting muscles in your midsection such as your abdominal muscles, 
lower back, and obliques. To perform weighted crunches, simply hold a dumbbell, kettlebell, 
or medicine ball as you perform a standard crunch.

Front dumbbell raise

Front dumbbell raises primarily target the front of the shoulders, 
known as the anterior deltoid. This muscle is used in shoulder flexion.
Front dumbbell raises also work the lateral (side) deltoid and the serratus anterior, 
along with the upper and lower trapezius, clavicular part of the pectoralis major, and biceps.

cable upright row 

An upright row is an effective exercise to build strength in the shoulders and upper back. 
It's a pull exercise, meaning you'll be pulling the weight toward you and targeting your posterior chain,
or the muscles on the backside of your body.

Flutter kicks

Flutter kicks activate your hip-flexor muscles,
lower-back muscles, and quads, while targeting your lower abdominal muscles 
more than other core workouts like sit-ups and crunches. With proper form, 
flutter kicks can increase your heart rate, promoting cardiovascular health.
